A Servant's Guide to the House

For Training New Staff
— Willem, Head Butler

The Daily Round

The house must be served in proper order, as the Mistress requires. Begin at dawn and follow this sequence without deviation.

First: The CHAPEL. Light the morning candles, air the incense, open the prayer book to the day's reading. The spirit must be tended before the body.

Second: The KITCHEN. Stoke the fires, begin the bread, set water to boil. A house runs on its stomach.

Third: The LIBRARY. Open curtains, dust the reading desk, check that no books were left open overnight (the Mistress is particular about this). The mind awakens with the day.

Fourth: The PARLOR. Lay the fire (do not light — that is the Mistress's preference), plump cushions, wind the clock. Guests may arrive at any hour.

Fifth: The DINING ROOM. Polish the silver, set for breakfast, arrange the flowers (if available). Presentation matters.

Sixth: The GALLERY. Check for dust (especially the frames), ensure curtains are drawn against the morning sun (the paintings must be protected). The ancestors watch always.

Seventh: The MASTER BEDROOM. Last, and only when the Mistress has risen. Change linens, empty the basin, open windows briefly. Privacy above all.

Eighth: The STUDY. This room is never to be entered without summons. If the bell rings, attend immediately. Otherwise, the Study tends itself.

The round takes approximately four hours. By noon, the house is ready to receive.

On the Cards

We play to pass the time when duties are complete. Mira taught us a game where each card represents a room, and we must play them in the order of the round. It helps the new ones learn.

Ace of Spades — Chapel (the highest, the spirit)
Two of Hearts — Kitchen (the hearth, the warmth)
Three of Clubs — Library (the clover, for luck in learning)
Four of Diamonds — Parlor (the gem, for guests)
Five of Hearts — Dining Room (where we share)
Six of Spades — Gallery (the ancestors, serious as spades)
Seven of Diamonds — Master Bedroom (seven for rest, diamonds for the Mistress)
Eight of Clubs — Study (eight for completion, clubs because we never go there)

Play them in order, and the day goes smooth. Play them wrong, and Willem scolds.
